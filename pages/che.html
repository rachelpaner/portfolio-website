The Task

Design an interactive and innovative mobile information system that addresses the issues surrounding sustainable development and is centered around user needs and desires.

Background

The Che Café is one of UCSD's most iconic fixtures with its longtime commitment to fostering alternative thinking and social good. Today, the Che Cafe stands as a DIY venue and community-oriented space that currently offers mutual aid, study sessions, and show/event opportunities for all identities and ages. Given its rich history, bold messaging, and all-inclusive values, we felt that the Che Café deserved a website truly reflected the dynamic and unique nature of the space.

Identifying the Problem

After an initial client survey, we identified a few key issues with the original website:

Through our web research, we were able to create a a diagram of constraints, problems, and potential solutions, and brainstorm further ideas to tackle these issues.

Our findings were exemplified by this statement from the 2021 EV Consumer Behavior Report by Fuels Institute, “Factors influencing likeliness of PEV purchase in 2017- 2019 are Affordability, Time Saving, Charger Availability, Familiarity and Options.”

This helped us during the brainstorming process, as we were able to organize our ideas around these influences. For example, I proposed a feature that would match potential buyers with EVs based on their lifestyle and budget, which would later become one of the primary fixtures of our app.


Brainstorming on Figma

With these ideas in mind, we thought about...

How might we help prospective EV Buyers become more informed on technical and financial aspects of owning an EV in order to facilitate their transition from gas-powered to electric vehicles?

User Research

  1. Personas

    2. The eclectic nature of the Che Café attracts visitors from all walks of life, so we wanted to capture the identites within the Che community. We interviewed 11 Che Café visitors across a selection of events and times, from daytime study hours to nighttime indie concerts, and used our findings to build 3 personas that capture the frequent users of the space.

    Based on our online research and community observations, May represents our primary user base: the Middle Class Prospective Buyer, age 30-45. Stastically, they are starting families and have a commutable job, and thus are on the market for a reliable family vehicle. However, they may be wary of the high price tag associated with EVs and be more comfortable with operating gas vehicles

    Jay represents our secondary user base: the Upper Class Prospective Buyer, age 20-35. Their higher salary affords them more financial freedom to choose an EV and the new technology may be more appealing to a younger generation. However, they may not view them as a first choice due to inconvenient charging times in a fast-paced, travel-heavy lifestyle.

    This demographic is also the most stastically likely to be Current EV Owners, another essential element of our secondary user base, with many being new buyers who might benefit from a community of individuals with similar vehicle experience.

  2. Storyboarding

    3. After identifying our stakeholders, we formulated storyboards that illustrated scenarios that our users might face by establishing the context, the problem, our solution, and the resolution.

    May is worried about the higher initial cost of EVs, so the financial plan feature helps May learn more about the costs and savings associated with EVs.
    Jay is unfamiliar and overwhelmed by the variety of EV options, so the the Car Match feature can help him find the perfect EV based on his lifestyle and budget preferences and minimizes research time.
    May is curious to find more information about EVs, so the Community feature connects with other prospective buyers and current EV owners to help answer questions and make suggestions.
    The Community Challenge feature encourages users to get involved through environment-based games and spread awareness about the benefits of sustainable transportation.

    Showing these storyboards during our user interviews allowed us to gain helpful feedback as to how realistic and applicable these situations and solutions were to daily life.

  3. User Interview Insights

    4. After conducting 7 user interviews (3 primary, 4 secondary), I took responsibility for all interview analysis and coding and discovered 3 key insights:

    1. Secondary users are more likely to be less informed about EVs and could benefit from guidance during the research process
    2. Environmental impact is not a significant factor in EV consideration
    3. Primary users are more likely to dismiss EVs because of high cost than secondary users

    These findings significantly changed the trajectory of the app, as we discovered that even though we were tackling an environmental issue, approaching the user from an environmentalist standpoint was not the most effective way .

    Instead, we leaned into the “economical over ecological” mindset of the Prospective Buyer in order to maximize efficiency for secondary users and increase financial transparency for primary users.

    Furthermore, we saw an expanded role and possibility of interaction for current EV owners, as those that we interviewed expressed the learning curve of operating an EV and that they often found themselves helping others at charging stations.


So, our solution evolved into...

EVolve, the one-stop-shop for sustainable transportation preparation

Our goal is to help the user create a curated financial plan, get matched with a car based on their lifestyle, and connect with a community of other prospective and current EV owners so that they can be more prepared and open to transitioning to an EV.

Style Guide

All Users

Onboarding Survey

  1. Input your preferred vehicle size, style, and price to determine suggestions for Car Match and Financial Plan features.
  2. Input the information of your current EV to show on your profile and find other drivers with similar vehicles


Prospective Buyers

Car Match

  1. Quickly pair with potential EV’s that fit your budget, lifestyle, and needs.
  2. Review key details about each car in a quick succession without needing to browse

Prospective Buyers

Budget Builder

  1. Record your monthly expenses to visualize your budget and spending habits
  2. Find personalized financial plans and payment estimates for cars that fit your desired budget and lifestyle


Prospective Buyers

View Nearby EVs

  1. Find EVs charging nearby and compare the cost of charging vs the cost of gas while fueling
  2. Check the financial info of the specific make and model of nearby EVs
  3. Chat with the owner of the EV to ask about their experience or get advice

Prospective Buyers

Gas Log/History

  1. Log the cost each time you fill up on gas and visually track your gas cost history
  2. Check gas cost percentage of your monthly spending habits and connect to the Budget Builder


Current Owners

Give/Get Help

  1. Connect with other EV drivers while charging
  2. Ask for help (e.g. charging questions, car trouble)
  3. Respond to help requests

Current Owners

Charging Log

Easily log new charging payments and keep track of charging and mileage history

All Users

EV Community

  1. Ask and search for EV related questions from other owners and prospective buyers
  2. Socialize through discussion and support others by replying to/interacting with posts

Reflection

Though EVolve was created for a course project, designing the app felt more like a labor of love. I thoroughly enjoyed learning more about the EV industry and community, and growing with the app through multiple iterations.

This project also taught me the importance of teamwork and communication. The difficulties in coordination, task delegation, and underlying frustrations we faced during our User Research stage led us to do some troubleshooting and discuss our dynamic as a team, which ultimately led to a much smoother and actively collaborative experience during the Prototyping stage.

What's Next?

I would ultimately like conduct usability testing using our hi-fi prototype on more 'Potential Buyer' and 'Current Owner' users, as the vehicle market is constantly changing and the app should reflect the most up-to-date needs and wants of our target audience.
